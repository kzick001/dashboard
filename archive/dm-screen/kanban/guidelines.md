Kanban Board Guidelines

1. The "Definition of Ready" (The Entry Gate)
To stop the "To-Do to Doing" dance, a project cannot enter the "Doing" column until it is Ready. Before moving a card to "Doing," ask:
Do I have all the materials/tools needed?
Do I have a clear "Micro-step" (15-min task) to start with?
Do I have the mental bandwidth for this specific type of work (Physical vs. Digital)?

2. How to Stop the "Back and Forth"
Moving cards back and forth is a symptom of Context Switching, which is the primary cause of maker burnout. Here are two guidelines to prevent it:
The "Tax" Rule Every time you move a card from "Doing" back to "To-Do" without completing it, you must "pay a tax": You cannot start a new project for 24 hours. This forces you to sit with the incompletion rather than chasing the dopamine of a new "Doing" card.
The "Start Finishing, Stop Starting" Mantra When you feel the urge to move a new card into "Doing," look at your current "Doing" cards and ask: "What is the absolute smallest thing I can do to move one of these to Done?" Completing a "Doing" task releases more dopamine than starting one, but starting one is easier. Kanban is the tool that forces you to choose the harder, more rewarding path.

3. Managing the "Done" Column
Don't clear your "Done" column immediately. At the end of the week, look at your "Done" pile. This is your "Sane-Check."
If "Done" is empty, your WIP limits are likely too high or your tasks are too big.
If "Done" is full, celebrate it. That is the proof that your system is protecting your time.
